What is Miru?

Most AI vision is a black box.
Miru is glass.

When an AI looks at a picture, it usually just spits out an answer with no explanation. Miru changes that. It shows you the AI's reasoning as it happens, highlights what it's looking at, and saves a record of every analysis so you can prove what the AI did — without ever keeping a copy of your image.

01

Vision AI, made transparent

You give Miru an image and a question. Miru hands the picture to a vision AI (an AI that can see and understand images), then translates what the AI is doing into plain reasoning steps you can read.

02

Live results, word by word

Instead of waiting for one big answer, you see the AI think out loud. Each reasoning step appears as it happens, like a conversation. You always know how far along it is.

03

An activity log you can audit

Every analysis is recorded — the question, the answer, the AI's reasoning, and a fingerprint of the image. The image itself is never stored. Useful when you need to prove what your AI saw and decided.

Privacy

Your image never leaves your control.

Only a fingerprint is logged — a one-way mathematical summary that proves "this was the same image" without keeping the image itself.

Image fingerprinting in plain English

When you analyze a picture, Miru calculates a SHA-256 fingerprint — a 64-character code that's unique to that exact image. Change one pixel and the code changes completely. From the code alone, no one can reconstruct the image. It's a one-way function: easy to compute, impossible to reverse.

Miru saves the fingerprint to the activity log and throws away the image. If the same image gets analyzed twice, the fingerprints match — so you can spot duplicates. But if someone steals your log file, they get a list of fingerprints and questions, never the pictures themselves.
